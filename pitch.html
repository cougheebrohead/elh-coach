A client who logs three meals a day is seven times less likely to churn. AI photo logging is the only nutrition entry mechanic that hits 70%+ adherence past day 30. MyFitnessPal manual entry hits 22% past day 30. The math is brutal — and the platform that owns this layer owns the retention.

What "4-second photo" actually does

  1. Detects every item on the plate

    Multi-item plates (chicken, rice, broccoli, sauce) — each item identified separately. Not "mixed plate, ~600 cal."

  2. Estimates portion sizes from visual cues

    Plate dimensions, food density, comparison to known reference items. Adjustable before saving.

  3. Returns calories + protein + carbs + fat

    Per item and total. Logged into the daily ring instantly.

  4. Learns from coach corrections

    Coach adjusts ("that wasn't half-cup, it was three-quarters") and the model improves per-client. After 2-3 weeks: barcode-grade accuracy on the foods that client actually eats.

  5. Cooking method aware

    Grilled vs fried vs baked — different macros. Auto-detected from photo cues, override-able.

  • Session attendance
  • Body comp trend
  • Cycle phase context (for female clients on opt-in)
  • Symptom log change pattern
